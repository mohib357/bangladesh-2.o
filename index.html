The Bangladesh Institute of Peace and Security Studies (BIPSS) recently organized a Policy Café at The Westin Dhaka, titled “Bangladesh 2.0: Reimagining our Economic Future,” gathering a diverse group of experts, business leaders, policymakers, and journalists to examine Bangladesh’s evolving economic landscape. Moderated by BIPSS President Major General ANM Muniruzzaman (retd), the event provided a platform to discuss Bangladesh’s current economic situation, including challenges such as inflation, income disparity, and trade imbalances, and explored potential policy paths toward a more resilient and forward-looking economy.

Bangladesh went through a bloody uprising in July, that led to the fall of Sheikh Hasina’s 15-year rule. Led by the youth, and a non-political platform, the uprising witnessed a moment of unprecedented unity. But the transition period has been fraught, and the interim government is dealing with long reform agenda:
re-establishing law and order, stabilising the economy and addressing various human rights abuse. People expect a ‘systemic’ change, which prevents a return to / or a reversal back to business as usual. What role can research institutes and academics play at this time of transition?

Bangladesh Launches New Satellite

Date: 12/01/2025 Time: 10.25 PM

The government-owned satellite operator in Bangladesh, Bangladesh Satellite Company Ltd (BSCL), intends to launch Bangabandhu Satellite-2 by 2023 under a G2G agreement with Russia. Bangladesh became the 57th country globally to join the club of satellites with the launch of Bangabandhu Satellite-1 into orbit in 2018.
On June 8, BSCL met with Prime Minister"s Information and Communication Technology Affairs Adviser Sajeeb Wazed to present Bangabandhu Satellite-2. The Bangabandhu Satellite-2 preliminary development project plan (PDPP) was submitted to the Planning Commission on October 4. Following policy approval, the PDPP will be forwarded to the Economic Relations Division (ERD) for negotiations with development partners.
